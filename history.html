History of the Team

Pakistan became an independent Test cricket nation in 1952, playing its first official Test match against India. Since then, the team has grown into one of the most exciting sides in world cricket, known for producing fast bowlers and unpredictable, thrilling performances.

Key Moments in Time

  • 1952 — Pakistan plays its first Test match, against India.
  • 1971 — Pakistan plays its first One Day International (ODI).
  • 1992 — Pakistan wins the Cricket World Cup in Australia under captain Imran Khan.
  • 1999 — Pakistan reaches the World Cup final, finishing as runners-up.
  • 2009 — Pakistan wins the ICC World Twenty20 in England.
  • 2017 — Pakistan wins the ICC Champions Trophy, beating India in the final.

Why the Team Matters

Cricket is more than a sport in Pakistan. It is a source of national pride. When the team plays, streets empty out and millions of fans gather around televisions to cheer for the Men in Green.
